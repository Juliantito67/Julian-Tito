⚡ HOW IT ALL STARTED

TORU IWATANI is a Japanese video game designer best known as the creator of PAC-MAN, one of the most iconic arcade games ever made. He joined NAMCO in the late 1970s and wanted to design a game different from violent "shooting" games popular at the time. Instead of focusing on combat, he aimed for something friendly and accessible to everyone.

That idea led to PAC-MAN, released in 1980, where players guide a yellow character through a maze eating pellets while avoiding ghosts. Each ghost has unique behavior, but all try to catch PAC-MAN. When PAC-MAN eats a ⚡ POWER PELLET ⚡, the ghosts turn blue and can be eaten for a short time!

🎮 The maze-chase phenomenon became a worldwide sensation, defining a generation of arcade culture.

🟡 GAME PLANS · PAC-MAN

PAC-MAN is a yellow, circle-shaped character with a mouth that opens and closes as he moves. In gameplay, he travels through a maze eating dots while avoiding ghosts. Controlled by the player, he’s always in motion, changing direction to navigate and survive. Eat power pellets to turn the tables on Blinky, Pinky, Inky & Clyde!
