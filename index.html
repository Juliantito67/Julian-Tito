LIVING LEGEND
◈ GAME DESIGN PIONEER ◈ Toru Iwatani is a Japanese video game designer best known as the creator of Pac-Man, one of the most famous arcade games ever made. He joined Namco in the late 1970s and wanted to design a game that was different from the violent "shooting" games that were popular. Instead of focusing on combat, he aimed for something more friendly and accessible to everyone. That idea led to Pac-Man, released in 1980, where players guide a character through a maze eating dots while avoiding ghosts. His vision broke barriers and defined arcade culture.
🕹️ Iwatani's philosophy: "a game about eating, not killing". He wanted to attract female players and couples to arcades. Pac-Man became a pop culture icon.
◈ LEGACY & INFLUENCE ◈
Toru Iwatani received the Lifetime Achievement Award at the Game Developers Choice Awards. Pac-Man is recognized by Guinness World Records as the most successful coin-operated arcade machine in history.

HOW TO PLAY

GAME PLANS · PAC-MAN PROTOCOL

▸ MAZE SURVIVAL GUIDE
PAC-MAN is a yellow, circle-shaped character with a mouth that opens and closes as he moves. In gameplay, he travels through a maze eating dots while avoiding ghosts. He is controlled by the player and is always in motion, changing direction to navigate the maze and survive.
🎮 CONTROLS: Joystick (4 directions) · Goal: clear all dots and avoid ghost contact. Eat Power Pellets (large flashing dots) to turn ghosts blue and eat them for extra points. Fruit bonuses appear below the maze for high scores.
◈ ARCADE TIPS ◈
  • Memorize ghost patterns: Blinky chases, Pinky ambushes, Inky flanks, Clyde wanders.
  • Use tunnels to warp to opposite side of maze.
  • Each Power Pellet gives about 6 seconds of blue ghost vulnerability.
  • Eat ghosts in sequence: 200, 400, 800, 1600 points.
  • Fruit items appear at specific dot thresholds: Cherry, Strawberry, Orange, Apple, etc.
