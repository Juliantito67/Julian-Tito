HOW IT ALL STARTED

1980 · revolution

🎮 TORU IWATANI

Toru Iwatani is a legendary Japanese video game designer, best known as the visionary creator of Pac-Man — one of the most iconic arcade games ever made. He joined Namco in the late 1970s with a bold mission: to break away from violent "shooting" games that dominated arcades. Instead of combat, Iwatani wanted to craft something friendly, charming, and accessible to everyone — including women and couples. That revolutionary idea gave birth to Pac-Man in 1980, a game where players guide a hungry yellow character through a maze, eating dots while outsmarting ghosts.

💡 "I wanted to create a game that everyone could enjoy, not just the hardcore shooter fans." — Toru Iwatani

⭐ BIRTH OF A LEGEND

Released in Japan in May 1980, Pac-Man (originally "Puck-Man") took the world by storm. Its non-violent, maze-chase gameplay, combined with charming characters, redefined arcade culture. Pac-Man became a global phenomenon, generating billions in revenue and inspiring countless sequels, merchandise, and even a cartoon. The game's success proved that video games could be fun for all ages, forever changing the industry landscape.

✨ First video game character to achieve mainstream pop culture icon status.

GAME PLAY  |  PAC-MAN

🟡 THE MAZE · RULES

In the classic maze, players control PAC-MAN, a round yellow hero with a dynamic chomping mouth. The goal is straightforward yet addictive: eat all the pellets (small dots) scattered across the maze while avoiding four relentless ghosts. Once all dots are cleared, the round advances. But danger lurks — touching any ghost means losing a life.

⚡ Power Pellets (large flashing dots) are game-changers: when Pac-Man eats one, all ghosts turn deep blue and become vulnerable for a short time. During this window, Pac-Man can devour them for bonus points, sending them back to the ghost house to respawn. Strategy and timing are everything!

🟡 PAC-MAN · THE ICON

Pac-Man is a cheerful, pizza-like yellow circle with a distinct mouth that opens and closes rhythmically as he moves through the neon labyrinth. He is controlled directly by the player and is always in motion — changing direction on command to navigate tight corridors, escape ambushes, and gobble pellets. Despite his simple design, Pac-Man's personality shines through his voracious appetite and underdog charm. He represents fun, persistence, and a touch of mischief.

🎮 Trivia: The character was inspired by a pizza missing a slice. Iwatani's vision was to create something warm and friendly, far from aggressive space invaders.

CHARACTERS · THE GHOSTS

fearsome foursome

In Pac-Man, the ghosts are the primary antagonists that relentlessly hunt the player through the twisting maze. Each ghost has a distinct personality, behavior, and chase pattern, making every encounter unique. Known as the Ghost Gang, they work together to corner Pac-Man. But when Pac-Man consumes a Power Pellet, they turn blue and edible — turning the tables briefly. Here are the four iconic ghosts:

👹 BLINKY
The red chaser
Aggressive — always directly targets Pac-Man's position. The leader of the pack.
🌸 PINKY
The pink ambusher
Aims for a spot ahead of Pac-Man, trying to cut him off.
🌀 INKY
The cyan trickster
Uses complex dual-position targeting — unpredictable and sneaky.
🍊 CLYDE
The orange wanderer
Changes behavior — chases when far, but flees when too close.
⚡ Power Pellet effect: When Pac-Man eats a Power Pellet, all ghosts turn deep blue and reverse direction. Eating a blue ghost awards increasing points (200/400/800/1600). After a few seconds, ghosts flash white, then revert to their dangerous forms. This high-risk, high-reward mechanic defines classic Pac-Man strategy.

LEGACY & ULTIMATE TRIVIA

📀 GLOBAL IMPACT

  • Over 400,000 arcade cabinets sold worldwide
  • First video game with a power-up mechanic (Power Pellet)
  • Guinness World Records: "Most Successful Coin-Operated Game"
  • Inducted into the World Video Game Hall of Fame
  • Inspired music, movies, cereal, and an animated series (1982)

🕹️ GAMEPLAY SECRETS

  • Ghost patterns: Each ghost has unique "scatter" and "chase" modes
  • The famous 256th level glitch (split-screen) — ultimate kill screen
  • Original name "Puck-Man" was changed to avoid vandalism
  • Ms. Pac-Man introduced more randomized ghost movement
  • Pac-Man's mouth direction influences hitbox precision
🎵 WAKA WAKA WAKA 🎵
The iconic sound of Pac-Man munching dots is one of the most recognizable audio cues in gaming history — created by sound designer Toshio Kai.
