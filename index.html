HOW IT ALL STARTED

Toru Iwatani

Creator · Designer · Visionary

TORU IWATANI is a Japanese video game designer best known as the creator of Pac-Man, one of the most famous arcade games ever made. He joined Namco in the late 1970s and wanted to design a game that was different from the violent "shooting" games that were popular at the time. Instead of focusing on combat, he aimed for something more friendly and accessible to everyone. That idea led to Pac-Man, released in 1980, where players guide a character through a maze eating dots while avoiding ghosts.

"I wanted to create a game that would appeal to women and couples, something joyful and non-violent."

GAME PLANS

PAC-MAN

yellow · circle · hungry

PAC-MAN is a yellow, circle-shaped character with a mouth that opens and closes as he moves. In gameplay, he travels through a maze eating dots while avoiding ghosts. He is controlled by the player and is always in motion, changing direction to navigate the maze and survive. When Pac-Man eats a Power Pellet, ghosts become vulnerable for a short time — turning the tables into a thrilling chase!

ARCADE REVOLUTION

When Pac-Man hit arcades in Japan (May 1980) and worldwide, it became a cultural phenomenon. It generated over $2.5 billion in quarters by the 1990s and inspired countless sequels, merchandise, and even a top-10 hit song "Pac-Man Fever". The maze design, iconic ghosts, and pellet-chomping gameplay defined a generation.

MODERN LEGACY

From Google Doodles to VR experiences, Pac-Man remains one of the most recognizable video game characters. In 2022, it was inducted into the World Video Game Hall of Fame. The core "eat dots, avoid ghosts" formula continues to inspire indie games and pop culture references.

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

Toru Iwatani’s simple shapes and pastel colors were purposely chosen to appeal to wider audiences. The name "Puck-Man" (later Pac-Man) derived from Japanese onomatopoeia "paku-paku" meaning munching. The maze layout and ghost AI created emergent strategy that still feels fresh today.

⚡ ULTIMATE WIKI FACT

The original arcade cabinet featured a unique "joystick" and maze layout with 4 ghosts: Shadow, Speedy, Bashful, and Pokey.

Toru Iwatani later became a professor at Tokyo Polytechnic University.
